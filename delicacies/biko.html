Biko

Biko is the sticky-sweet star of every Filipino celebration! Made with glutinous rice, creamy coconut milk, and brown sugar, it's like a chewy hug in dessert form. Topped with a shiny layer of latik (toasted coconut curds) or syrupy glaze, biko is the perfect balance of rich and satisfying. Whether it's at a fiesta, birthday, or even a random merienda, it's that dependable dessert that always makes you say, “Isa pang slice, please!”

Ingredients:

For the Rice Base:

  • 2 cups glutinous rice
  • 4 cups water

For the Coconut Syrup:

  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 1/2 cups brown sugar
  • 1/2 tsp salt

For the Topping (Optional):

  • 1 cup coconut cream
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar

Recipe:

  1. Cook the Glutinous Rice:
    • Rinse the glutinous rice, then cook it in 4 cups of water. Use a rice cooker or cook over low heat until the water is absorbed and the rice is tender. Set aside.
  2. Make the Coconut Syrup:
    • In a large pan or pot, combine coconut milk, brown sugar, and salt.
    • Cook over medium heat, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens slightly.
  3. Combine Rice and Syrup:
    • Add the cooked rice to the syrup mixture.
    • Mix well until the rice absorbs the syrup and becomes sticky.
  4. Prepare the Topping (Optional):
    • In a separate pan, cook the coconut cream and brown sugar over medium heat.
    • Stir until it thickens into a golden-brown glaze.
  5. Assemble the Biko:
    • Transfer the sticky rice mixture into a greased or lined rectangular pan.
    • Flatten and smooth the surface.
    • Pour the topping evenly over the rice.
  6. Finish and Serve:
    • Let the biko cool and set for about 30 minutes.
    • Slice into squares and serve at room temperature.

Perfect for fiestas, birthdays, or anytime you're craving that classic Filipino sweetness!
