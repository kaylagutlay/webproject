Cassava Cake

Cassava Cake is the rich and creamy queen of Filipino kakanin! Made from grated cassava, coconut milk, and sweetened goodness, it's like a warm, chewy hug in every bite. Topped with a golden custard layer, it's the ultimate dessert for fiestas, family reunions, or whenever you want to treat yourself. It's that one dish that always disappears first at the table, leaving everyone asking, “May extra pa ba?” Simple, satisfying, and proudly Pinoy!

Ingredients:

For the Cake:

  • 2 cups grated cassava (fresh or frozen, thawed)
  • 1 cup coconut milk
  • 1 can (300ml) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (370ml) evaporated milk
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1/4 cup melted butter

For the Topping:

  • 1/2 cup coconut milk
  • 1/2 cup sweetened condensed milk
  • 2 egg yolks

Recipe:

  1. Preheat the Oven:
    • Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a rectangular or square baking pan.
  2. Mix the Cake Batter:
    • In a large bowl, combine grated cassava, coconut milk, condensed milk, evaporated milk, sugar, eggs, and melted butter. Mix until well combined.
  3. Bake the Cake:
    • Pour the mixture into the prepared baking pan.
    • Bake for 40–50 minutes or until the top is set and slightly golden.
  4. Prepare the Topping:
    • In a bowl, whisk together coconut milk, condensed milk, and egg yolks.
    • Once the cake is set, pour the topping evenly over the baked cassava.
  5. Broil for the Final Touch:
    • Return the cake to the oven and broil on the top rack for 10–15 minutes or until the topping turns golden brown. Watch carefully to avoid burning.
  6. Cool and Serve:
    • Let the cassava cake cool before slicing into squares. Serve warm or at room temperature.

Cassava cake is perfect for any occasion—sweet, creamy, and so irresistible!
