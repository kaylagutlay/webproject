Ube Halaya

Ube Halaya is the purple gem of Filipino desserts! Made from mashed purple yam, it's creamy, sweet, and oh-so-satisfying. With its rich, velvety texture and that distinct nutty-vanilla flavor, it's the kind of treat that feels like a warm hug from nanay. Whether it's spread on bread, topped on halo-halo, or eaten straight out of the jar (we won't judge!), ube halaya is a fan favorite that never goes out of style. It's the dessert that says, "Life's better in purple!"

Ingredients:

  • 1 kg ube (purple yam), boiled and mashed (or 2 cups ube powder, rehydrated)
  • 1 can (300ml) sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 can (370ml) evaporated milk
  • 1 cup coconut milk or cream
  • 1/2 cup sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 cup butter or margarine, melted
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Recipe:

  1. Prepare the Ube:
    • If using fresh ube, boil it until soft, peel, and mash thoroughly.
    • If using powdered ube, rehydrate it according to package instructions.
  2. Cook the Ube Mixture:
    • In a large, non-stick pan, combine the mashed ube, condensed milk, evaporated milk, and coconut milk.
    • Mix well to ensure all ingredients are combined.
  3. Sweeten and Flavor:
    • Add sugar and vanilla extract. Stir continuously over low to medium heat to prevent burning.
  4. Add Butter for Creaminess:
    • When the mixture starts to thicken, gradually add the melted butter.
    • Keep stirring until the mixture is thick and smooth, and starts to pull away from the sides of the pan (about 30â€“40 minutes).
  5. Cool and Serve:
    • Transfer the ube halaya into containers or molds greased with butter.
    • Let it cool completely and set before serving.

Enjoy as a dessert, spread, or topping! Ube halaya is perfect on its own or as the star ingredient in other Filipino treats.
