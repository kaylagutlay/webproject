Kutsinta

Kutsinta is the little brown pancake of Filipino snacks! Chewy, slightly sweet, and topped with freshly grated coconut, it's the humble yet lovable star of any merienda spread. With its jiggly, sticky goodness, kutsinta feels like a comforting throwback to childhood afternoons spent with family. Whether eaten on its own or paired with coffee, it's the kind of treat that always hits the spot. Plus, who doesn't love biting into that perfect balance of soft and coconutty? Kutsinta is proof that simple things are often the best!

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup cassava flour
  • 1 1/2 cups brown sugar
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 tsp lye water (*lihiya*)
  • 1 tsp annatto powder (or atsuete seeds, soaked in water for coloring)
  • Grated fresh coconut (for topping)

Recipe:

  1. Prepare the Steamer:
    • Fill your steamer with water and bring it to a boil. Grease small molds (like cupcake molds) lightly with oil to prevent sticking.
  2. Mix the Batter:
    • In a mixing bowl, combine all-purpose flour, cassava flour, and brown sugar. Mix well.
    • Gradually add water while stirring to dissolve the sugar completely.
    • Add lye water and annatto powder. Mix until the batter is smooth and free of lumps.
  3. Pour into Molds:
    • Strain the batter to remove any lumps.
    • Pour the mixture into prepared molds, filling each about 3/4 full.
  4. Steam the Kutsinta:
    • Place the molds in the steamer and cover with a clean kitchen towel under the lid to prevent water droplets from falling into the batter.
    • Steam for 30â€“40 minutes or until the kutsinta is firm and translucent.
  5. Cool and Serve:
    • Let the kutsinta cool for a few minutes before removing from the molds.
    • Serve with freshly grated coconut on top.

Kutsinta is perfect for merienda or paired with hot coffee or tea. Chewy, sweet, and oh-so-satisfying!
